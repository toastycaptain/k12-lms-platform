# IB Phase 9 Family and Student Trust and Launch Checklist

Covers task range `328` to `337`.

## What shipped
- Trust-policy model and service layer with audience, cadence, approval, privacy, and localization rules.
- Guardian and student surfaces now show explicit trust-policy panels rather than hiding calm-mode rules behind preference forms.
- Replacement-readiness output now includes trust as a first-class track in the final audit.

## Trust contract
- Policies can target `guardian`, `student`, or `mixed` audiences.
- Delivery summary ties receipts, acknowledgements, quiet-hours preferences, and cadence modes back into the trust layer.
- Trust framework statements are surfaced in-product so the communication policy is inspectable by operators and internal stakeholders.

## Why this matters
- Calm communication is now represented as policy, not just copy.
- Family and student surfaces gain an explicit explanation of cadence and approval logic without exposing staff-only operational detail.

## Remaining explicit gaps
- Accessibility and privacy checklists remain documented rather than persisted as pass/fail records.
- Trust telemetry does not yet feed a dedicated support or incident dashboard.
